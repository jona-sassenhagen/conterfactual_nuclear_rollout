What If Germany Had Not Stopped Building Nuclear Power Plants?

Counterfactual Nuclear Roll-Out

A counterfactual scenario where Germany continuously builds reactors at the pace it did in the 1980s, approximately three reactors every two years. Demand is taken from historical data. Renewables are kept static at 1998 levels. Fossil fuel plants are incrementally shut down: new reactors replace fossil sites as soon as equivalent baseload becomes available.
